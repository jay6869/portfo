The SOC Platform exists because every time I set up a teaching lab I was redoing the same
two days of YAML. The components are all excellent on their own — the friction is entirely
in the seams between them.

## The glue is the product

The interesting engineering isn't Wazuh or the scanner; it's the **webhook bridge** that
translates a scan finding into a Wazuh custom rule, so a discovered service immediately
sharpens detection instead of sitting in a separate report.

```yaml title="docker-compose.excerpt.yml"
services:
  wazuh-manager:
    image: wazuh/wazuh-manager:4.x
    depends_on: [indexer]
  scanner-api:
    build: ./scanner
    environment:
      WAZUH_WEBHOOK: http://wazuh-manager:55000/rules/sync
```

## What it taught me

Most of the value for students was watching the feedback loop close in real time — scan a
host, see the asset appear, watch a tuned rule fire on the next event. That loop is the
whole pitch of purple teaming, compressed into eight minutes.
